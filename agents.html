SPEC:003 // agent roster

Six systems we build for firms that are tired of AI living in random tabs

We do not start with "everything." We start with the workflow that keeps slipping, dragging, or getting rebuilt by hand. These are the first systems we reach for most often.

  • proposal
    Proposal System
    Turns scattered notes, call transcripts, old scopes, and pricing rules into a repeatable proposal workflow. Faster turnaround, less blank-page work, fewer inconsistencies between what was promised and what gets delivered.
  • reporting
    Client Reporting System
    Pulls numbers from the tools you already use, drafts the report, flags missing context, and gives the team a clean review point before anything goes to the client.
  • follow-up
    Follow-Up System
    Keeps leads, prospects, and clients from going cold because the next message depended on someone remembering to send it. Cadence, triggers, approvals, and handoff rules live in one dependable system.
  • research
    Research System
    Stops the team from redoing the same account research, market scans, and meeting prep every week. It gathers context, structures it, and hands back something usable instead of another pile of tabs.
  • delivery
    Delivery Coordination System
    Routes work between intake, approvals, execution, and client delivery so tasks stop disappearing in Slack, email, and personal to-do lists. Useful when delivery is strong but internal coordination keeps leaking time.
  • inbox
    Internal Inbox System
    Monitors incoming requests, triages them, summarizes what matters, and keeps the right person from becoming the human router for every message, task, and exception.

SPEC:005 // process

How the rebuild works

The process is short on purpose. The goal is a working system your team can inherit, not a long diagnosis phase.

  1. Map the workflow that keeps slipping

    We identify where the team is still improvising: proposal assembly, reporting prep, follow-up, research, or delivery coordination. Then we define the trigger, inputs, approvals, and handoff.

  2. We build and test it on your stack

    The system gets wired into the tools you already use, with the right guardrails and approval logic. Before handoff, we test the actual workflow rather than showing you a polished mockup.

  3. Your team inherits it cleanly

    You get documentation, operating guidance, and a clear answer to what happens if something needs review, adjustment, or expansion. The system should survive after the initial engagement, not depend on us forever.
