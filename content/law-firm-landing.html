LAND:001.2 // the intake problem

The Workflow Problem Every Firm Has

Most law firms run on manual intake, manual follow-up, and manual document collection. That works when the firm is small enough for one person to hold it all in their head. It stops working the moment volume picks up or someone takes a day off.

A potential client reaches out at 11pm after a DUI arrest. An existing client was supposed to send their medical records last week and hasn't. A consultation was booked, but nobody followed up with the pre-meeting questionnaire. A new PI lead came in three days ago, and the follow-up email never went out.

According to the ABA, 35% of potential clients who don't get a response on their first try never reach out again. For a PI firm, that's not a missed message — it's a missed $15,000 to $100,000 case. And intake is only the beginning. Document collection, case research, and reporting all leak time the same way.

LAND:001.3 // capabilities

What AI Native Does for Your Firm

We find the workflow that bleeds the most time and make it dependable. For most law firms, that starts with intake and follow-up — then expands to document collection, research, and reporting.

  • 01
    Intake qualification that runs without you
    New inquiries get qualified automatically — practice area, timeline, key facts, urgency. You get a structured summary with everything you need to decide if the case is worth pursuing, before you follow up.
  • 02
    Follow-up that doesn't depend on someone remembering
    New lead came in Tuesday, no response by Thursday? The system follows up. Consultation booked but client hasn't confirmed? Reminder goes out. No leads go cold because the next step depended on someone's to-do list.
  • 03
    Document collection that stays on track
    Medical records, police reports, financial disclosures, signed retainers — the system tracks what's outstanding, sends reminders on a cadence, and flags what's overdue. Your team stops chasing documents manually.
  • 04
    Case research that doesn't start from scratch
    Statute lookups, precedent research, jurisdiction-specific procedures — structured and reusable instead of rebuilt every time. Your attorneys spend less time on setup and more on strategy.
  • 05
    Reporting that assembles itself
    Case status updates, billing summaries, pipeline reports. The system pulls the data, drafts the report, and gives you a review point. No more rebuilding the same spreadsheet every week.

Real Scenarios

Saturday night, 11:30pm — DUI arrest

A man just got arrested for DUI. His wife fills out your contact form. Your office is closed.

Without AI Native: The inquiry sits until Monday. She contacts two more firms. One responds. They get the retainer.

With AI Native: The intake workflow qualifies the lead automatically — arrest time, location, court date. A follow-up sequence starts. Monday morning, the structured summary is in your inbox. You call first.

Wednesday, 5:45pm — Car accident

A woman was rear-ended 20 minutes ago. She's Googling "personal injury lawyer near me" and reaching out to the first three results.

Without AI Native: Your office closed at 5. Her inquiry sits. The second firm responds. Case value: $35,000.

With AI Native: The intake system captures injury details, at-fault driver info, and police report number. Follow-up is queued. A consultation gets booked for the next morning. Without AI Native: A paralegal realizes the gap during case prep. Now it's a rush. The deadline is in five days.

With AI Native: The document collection workflow flagged the missing records at day 7, sent a reminder at day 10, and escalated to your team at day 14. The records arrived yesterday.
