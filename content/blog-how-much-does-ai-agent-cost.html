Setup Costs: What You Pay Upfront

DIY with no-code platforms

Platforms like Make.com and Zapier let you build automations visually. Upfront dollar cost: essentially zero. But the real cost is your time.

  • Learning the platform: 10-20 hours
  • Building your first agent workflow: 15-30 hours
  • Getting it production-ready: 10-20 hours (error handling, edge cases, testing)
  • Total time investment: 35-70 hours per agent

At $50/hour opportunity cost, that's $1,750-3,500 per agent in time — even though you didn't write a check.

DIY with open-source tools

Self-hosted tools like n8n eliminate per-operation fees. More powerful, more complex.

  • Server setup and configuration: 5-10 hours
  • Learning the framework: 15-25 hours
  • Building agents: 20-40 hours per agent
  • Total time: 40-75 hours for your first agent

Higher time investment, but zero platform fees and no per-operation limits. Better for the long run if you have technical skills.

SaaS AI tools

Pre-built AI tools with subscription pricing. Setup varies wildly:

  • Self-serve (basic): $0 setup, 1-2 hours of configuration
  • Guided onboarding: $500-2,000 setup fee, 2-5 hours of your time
  • Enterprise onboarding: $2,000-10,000 setup, weeks of meetings

Custom development agency

A software agency builds custom AI agents to your specifications.

  • Discovery and scoping: $2,000-5,000 (2-4 weeks)
  • Development: $5,000-30,000 (4-12 weeks)
  • Testing and deployment: $1,000-5,000 (1-3 weeks)
  • Total: $8,000-40,000+

Agencies build custom everything. That's powerful but expensive, and timelines often slip. A "6-week project" regularly becomes 12.

Done-for-you specialist (Milo model)

  • 1-2 agent setup: $399 (2-3 days delivery)
  • Full agent team (5-6 agents): $2,499 (4-5 days delivery)
  • Your time investment: 2-3 hours (kickoff call + review)

How is this so much cheaper than an agency? Specialization. We've built the same patterns — receptionist agents, lead follow-up, scheduling coordination — dozens of times. The architecture is proven. We're not scoping from scratch; we're configuring a battle-tested system for your business.

Monthly Costs: What Keeps Agents Running

Every AI agent has three ongoing costs:

1. AI API costs (the model's brain)

Your agent needs an LLM (Large Language Model) to think. You pay per use.

Usage Level Monthly Conversations Approx. API Cost
Light 100-500 $5-15
Medium 500-2,000 $15-40
Heavy 2,000-10,000 $40-120
Very Heavy 10,000+ $120-300+

Most small businesses fall in the light-to-medium range. API costs have dropped 90%+ since 2024 and continue falling.

2. Hosting (the agent's body)

Your agent needs somewhere to run.

  • SaaS tools: Included in subscription (you're paying for it in the monthly fee)
  • Self-hosted (VPS): $5-15/month (DigitalOcean, Hetzner, Railway)
  • Cloud functions (serverless): $0-10/month for typical usage

3. Communication channels

If your agent handles phone calls, SMS, WhatsApp, or email:

  • Phone/SMS (Twilio): $1/month per number + $0.01-0.04 per minute/message
  • WhatsApp Business API: $0-15/month depending on volume
  • Email (Resend, SendGrid): Free tier covers most small businesses

💡 Typical monthly cost for a 2-3 agent small business setup

Hosting: $10-15

Communication channels: $5-15

Total: $30-65/month

Hidden Costs That Change the Math

The cost nobody budgets for: maintenance

Every AI system needs tuning over time. Prompts need updating. Edge cases emerge. New scenarios arise. Budget 1-3 hours per month for oversight — whether that's your time or someone else's.

SaaS-specific hidden costs

  • Overage charges: Go over your plan limits and costs spike 2-5x per unit
  • Integration fees: Many charge extra for CRM, email, or calendar integrations
  • Annual price increases: 8-15% annually is standard for B2B SaaS
  • Exit costs: Data export fees, workflow recreation time, team retraining

DIY-specific hidden costs

  • Debugging time: When something breaks at 9pm on a Tuesday, you're the support team
  • Opportunity cost: Every hour spent maintaining agents is an hour not spent on revenue-generating work
  • Platform risk: Make.com changes pricing? Zapier deprecates an integration? You're rebuilding.
