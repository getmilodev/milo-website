How AI Phone Answering Actually Works (30-Second Version)

A caller dials your business number. Instead of voicemail, an AI voice answers — using your business name, your greeting, your rules. Depending on the service, it can:

  • Answer questions about your hours, services, location, pricing
  • Book appointments directly into your calendar (Google, Calendly, etc.)
  • Qualify leads — ask what they need, how urgent it is, collect their info
  • Route urgent calls to your cell or on-call staff
  • Send summaries via text/email after every call

The voice quality in 2026 is genuinely good. Most callers don't realize they're talking to AI for the first 15-20 seconds. Some never realize it at all.

The 3 Pricing Models (One of Them Costs 10x More Than You Think)

Model 1: Per-call or per-minute (the trap)

You pay a base fee, then extra for every call or minute. Sounds reasonable at low volumes. But here's the math nobody shows you:

A dental practice getting 150 calls/month at $9/call overage on a 20-call base plan: $210 + (130 × $9) = $1,380/month. That "$210/month" service is actually costing you $16,560/year.

Model 2: Flat monthly subscription

Predictable monthly fee regardless of call volume. Better than per-call, but you're renting — paying forever, owning nothing. Cancel and it all disappears.

Model 3: One-time setup (ownership)

Pay once. The AI answering system gets deployed on your infrastructure. You own it. Ongoing costs are just hosting and AI API usage — typically $25-50/month. Only one provider currently offers this model.

⚠️ The 3-year reality check

Over 3 years, a $99/month service costs $3,564. A per-call service at moderate volume costs $15,000-$50,000. The one-time ownership model costs roughly $1,900 total. Same AI. Same result. 5-25x price difference.

7 Questions to Ask Before You Buy

  1. What's the real cost at MY call volume? Don't look at the starting price. Calculate with your actual calls/month. Check last month's phone records if you're not sure.
  2. What happens when I cancel? With SaaS services, everything disappears. With ownership models, your system keeps running. Know which you're signing up for.
  3. Can it book appointments? "Yes" means different things. Some just capture the request. Others actually check your calendar and confirm a slot. Test the difference.
  4. How does it handle calls it can't answer? Good services route to your cell. Bad ones just say "please call back during business hours." Test the fallback.
  5. Is there a per-call or per-minute surcharge? If yes, model your busiest month, not your average month. That spike is where surprise bills come from.
  6. Can callers tell it's AI? Ask for a demo call. Better yet, have someone who doesn't know call the demo line and ask them afterward. Voice quality varies hugely between providers.
  7. How long until it's actually working? Some services activate in minutes. Others take days to configure properly. "Quick setup" can mean "generic setup" — make sure customization is included.
