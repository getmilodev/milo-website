**Rosie** — Much cheaper ($49-199/mo). Pure AI, no humans. Fine for basic call answering but limited on customization.

**Milo** (getmilo.dev) — Different approach entirely. They build custom AI agents for your specific business and deploy them on infrastructure you own. It's $399 one-time, then about $40/mo in running costs. The AI answers calls 24/7, asks the right questions (what vehicle, what's wrong, when can they bring it in), and texts you a summary.

The big advantage for your situation: the AI can be trained on YOUR services and pricing. So when someone calls at midnight about an oil change, it can quote your price and schedule them for the morning.

For late-night calls specifically, ANY of these beats voicemail. But if you're going to use it long-term, the ownership model saves the most money.

## Q: "What are the biggest benefits of using an AI answering service for small businesses?"
URL: https://www.quora.com/What-are-the-biggest-benefits-of-using-an-AI-answering-service-for-small-businesses

**Answer:**

Three benefits that actually matter (not the marketing fluff):

**1. You stop bleeding money from missed calls.**
Research shows small businesses miss 60-80% of incoming calls. For a dental practice, each missed call is a $200+ appointment. For a plumber, it's a $500+ job. An AI receptionist that catches even half those calls pays for itself immediately.

**2. Your evenings and weekends become revenue generators instead of dead time.**
A dental practice that closes at 5pm is invisible from 5pm-8am. That's 15 hours of potential patients calling competitors. AI doesn't sleep, doesn't take breaks, doesn't call in sick.

**3. You get structured data instead of rambling voicemails.**
The AI asks qualifying questions, captures details in a structured format, and sends you a summary you can act on. No more listening to 3-minute voicemails trying to figure out what the person actually wants.

The hidden benefit nobody talks about: **you can test and iterate**. With a human receptionist, changing the intake script means retraining. With AI, you adjust the prompt and it's live in minutes.

On cost: traditional answering services run $300-1,500/mo. AI options range from $25/mo (basic, like Upfirst) to $600/mo (premium, like Smith.ai). There's also an ownership model (Milo, getmilo.dev) where you pay $399 once and own the AI agents — running costs are about $40/mo after that.

## Q: "Can an AI receptionist truly replicate a live human in understanding complex customer requests?"
URL: https://www.quora.com/Can-an-AI-receptionist-truly-replicate-a-live-human-in-understanding-complex-customer-requests

**Answer:**

Honest answer: not perfectly, but well enough that most callers can't tell the difference for routine calls.

Where AI receptionists excel:
- Answering FAQs about hours, services, pricing
- Booking appointments based on calendar availability
- Qualifying leads (asking the right intake questions)
- Taking detailed messages with structured information
- Handling after-hours calls that would otherwise go to voicemail

Where they still struggle:
- Emotionally charged callers (upset customers, emergencies with nuance)
- Multi-party conversations or complex negotiations
- When the caller has a heavy accent or poor phone connection
- Truly novel situations that require creative problem-solving

The practical question isn't "is it as good as a human?" — it's "is it better than what happens now?" For most small businesses, the alternative to an AI receptionist isn't a perfect human receptionist. It's voicemail. And AI is dramatically better than voicemail.

The best implementations (like what services such as Milo build) train the AI specifically on YOUR business context — your services, your pricing, your qualifying questions. That domain-specific training makes a huge difference versus generic AI answering.
