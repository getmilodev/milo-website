## "Why we charge $399 once instead of $600/mo"

We spent 2 years running AI agents for ourselves before turning it into a business.

Here's what we learned: the subscription model for AI tools is broken for small businesses.

Smith.ai charges $600/mo. Ruby charges $500/mo. Rosie charges $199/mo.

Over 3 years, a dental practice pays $21,600 to Smith.ai for something that costs $40/mo to actually run.

The AI models cost pennies per call. Twilio charges cents per minute. The actual infrastructure cost for 24/7 AI phone answering is about $40/month.

The other $560/mo? That's rent. You're paying it forever and own nothing.

So we built Milo. We charge $399 to build and deploy AI agents on YOUR infrastructure. Your Twilio account, your AI keys, your data. After setup, running costs are ~$40/mo paid directly to the providers.

Cancel us, keep everything. The agents don't stop working because you stopped paying us.

It's a worse business model for us (no recurring revenue). But it's a dramatically better deal for the business owner.

We've done setups for dental practices, law firms, and HVAC companies. The average customer saves $6,000+ in year one vs. subscription alternatives.
